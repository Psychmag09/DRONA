Derailment

when a train or a railway vehicle leaves its tracks, causing it to lose stability and potentially leading to an accident. It occurs when the wheels of the train are no longer in contact with the rails, causing the train to either partially or completely come off the tracks.

solving the problem of train derailment can have significant positive impacts. It can improve public safety, increase efficiency and reliability of transportation, reduce economic losses and environmental damage, and enhance the reputation of the rail industry.

Current Solutions

  • Inspection Based

    Regular maintenance and inspection of rail tracks, rolling stock, and other railway infrastructure to identify and address any issues before they can cause Derailment.

  • Laser Based

    Laser-based inspection uses lasers to scan the surface of rail tracks and other components and create a detailed 3D image. The image can then be analyzed to detect any anamolies.

  • Reactive Measures

    Involve emergency brakes, derailment containment devices, and rescue and evacuation procedures. These measures are taken after the derailment has occurred to minimize damage.
